## Available Simulations

### 1. Projectile Motion (Physics)
**Concepts:** Kinematics, gravity, vectors, trajectories
**Best For:** Grades 9-12
**Activities:**
- Have students predict maximum height for different angles
- Compare trajectories at different velocities
- Explore how gravity affects motion
- Calculate range for optimal launch angles

### 2. pH Scale Explorer (Chemistry)
**Concepts:** Acids, bases, pH scale, chemical properties
**Best For:** Grades 6-12
**Activities:**
- Identify common acids and bases
- Compare pH values of household items
- Understand the logarithmic pH scale
- Discuss applications in daily life

### 3. Cell Division (Biology)
**Concepts:** Mitosis, cell reproduction, chromosomes
**Best For:** Grades 7-12
**Activities:**
- Learn the stages of mitosis in order
- Identify key features of each stage
- Discuss the importance of cell division
- Draw and label each stage

### 4. Wave Motion (Physics)

## Classroom Tips

### For Remote Learning
- Share the website link in your LMS (Canvas, Google Classroom, etc.)
- Assign specific simulations as homework
- Use simulations during video calls for demonstrations

### For In-Person Classes
- Display simulations on a projector or smartboard
- Have students work in pairs on computers/tablets
- Use as lab station activities
- Incorporate into lesson plans

### For Flipped Classroom
- Assign simulations as pre-class activities
- Have students answer questions about their observations
- Discuss findings during class time
- Build on simulation concepts with deeper material

## Assessment Ideas

1. **Lab Reports:** Have students write reports on their observations
2. **Data Collection:** Students record values from simulations
3. **Predictions:** Students predict outcomes before testing
4. **Comparisons:** Compare simulation results with real experiments
5. **Presentations:** Students present findings to the class
