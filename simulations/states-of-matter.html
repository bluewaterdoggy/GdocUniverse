Liquid State

About States of Matter:

Matter exists in different states depending on temperature and pressure. The three common states are:

  • Solid (below 0¬∞C for water): Particles vibrate in fixed positions. Definite shape and volume.
  • Liquid (0¬∞C to 100¬∞C for water): Particles can move past each other. Definite volume but takes container's shape.
  • Gas (above 100¬∞C for water): Particles move freely and quickly. No definite shape or volume, expands to fill container.

  • Freezing: Liquid ‚Üí Solid (removing heat)
