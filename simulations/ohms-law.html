About Ohm's Law:

Ohm's Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit:

  • Voltage (V): The electrical potential difference, measured in Volts (V). Think of it as electrical "pressure".
  • Current (I): The flow of electric charge, measured in Amperes (A). This is the rate of charge flow.
  • Resistance (R): Opposition to current flow, measured in Ohms (Œ©). Materials with higher resistance restrict current more.

The formulas:

  • V = I √ó R (Voltage equals Current times Resistance)
  • I = V / R (Current equals Voltage divided by Resistance)
  • R = V / I (Resistance equals Voltage divided by Current)
